The Distribution of Mimbres Pottery

Welcome to the website for the Mimbres Pottery StoryMap! This is the landing page for the project and it contains some background information on the project and more resources on the topic.

Background

The practice of archaeology creates massive amounts of data including physical artifacts and their accompanying photographs and written reports, digital collections databases with information on where and how artifacts are stored and thier quality of preservation, and geospatial datasets including shapefiles and remote sensing data. The organization of and access to these data are thus highly important to keep the field running smoothly. Moreover, the dissemination of information within academic circles and in the general public is becoming the new norm. Platfoms like tDAR, aka the Digital Archaeological Record, allow for easy data management for the owners and easy access to the general public, revolutionizing the way that archaeological information is shared. Instead of travelling to museums and having to pay a hefty fee for a ticket, individuals can now long on to tDAR and explore collections they would never have had access to otherwise and academia no longer sees the same amount of data hoarding by a single academic or organization. This project exemplifies the potentials of this new wave of dissemination by using data from the Mimbres Pottery Images Digital Database, an open-access dataset housed in tDAR.
